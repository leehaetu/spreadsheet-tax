The problem

Landlords often keep rent ledgers in spreadsheets. Quarterly MTD updates force either retyping or trusting a system that hides how figures were mapped. Joint ownership and overseas property make that worse.

Vs a full property ledger product: keep your rent sheet as the day-to-day record, then map one quarter for review — including UK and foreign property when you need both.

How it works

  1. Keep rental income and costs in your spreadsheet (or our free template).
  2. Create a free account and upload the quarter.
  3. Check UK and foreign property totals before approve.
  4. Connect HMRC and send when you are ready.

What you’ll need

Your National Insurance number, the tax year, and property details from HMRC once you connect. Start with our free template if your current sheet is messy.

Common questions

  • UK and foreign property?

    Yes — both can be reviewed in the product. Use clear country codes for foreign property.

  • Joint ownership?

    Record the share you need to report. Check figures carefully before you approve a send.

    No. Free to start; experimental paid prices are labelled on Pricing.
