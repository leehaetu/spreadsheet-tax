The problem

Making Tax Digital means quarterly updates. Many sole traders already track takings and costs in Excel or CSV — then waste time retyping into another system, or rush a messy file at the last minute.

Vs retyping into another system: keep day-to-day books in your spreadsheet, then upload one quarter for a plain-English check — no full ledger migration.

How it works

  1. Keep your books in your spreadsheet (or use our free template).
  2. Create a free account and upload the quarter.
  3. Check this period and year-to-date totals on screen.
  4. Approve, then connect HMRC when you are ready to send.

Common questions

  • Do I need to leave my spreadsheet?

    No. Day-to-day books stay in your file. Upload a period summary for the quarter you are reporting.

  • Is this free?

    Yes to start. Paid plans are not available to buy online yet — see Pricing.

  • Is Spreadsheet Tax HMRC-recognised?

    No. Independent software, not on HMRC’s recognised list yet. You approve every figure before send.

  • What if I also have rental income?

    You can include property in a combined file or use the landlord path.
