Help

How Spreadsheet Tax works with HMRC. This is the in-app guide — not the public sales site.

Income sources come from HMRC

Spreadsheet Tax is bridging software. It mirrors the self-employment and property businesses already registered with HMRC. It does not create, replace, or delete HMRC businesses.

  • After you connect HMRC, use Set up to load businesses from HMRC.
  • You can give each business a display name so it is easy to recognise in quarterly updates.
  • You cannot invent a trade or property business inside Spreadsheet Tax.

If a business is missing

  1. Sign in to your HMRC online account.
  2. Add or update the income source with HMRC (self-employment or property), following HMRC’s process.
  3. Return to Spreadsheet Tax and open Set up (or Sources → Refresh from HMRC).
  4. Choose Load businesses from HMRC again so the list updates.

Official guidance: Add or cease income sources (GOV.UK).

Quarterly updates

  1. Connect HMRC and load your income sources.
  2. Open Quarterly updates and choose one income source.
  3. Upload a spreadsheet covering this quarter’s figures for that source.
  4. Check the mapped figures, then review and send for that source only.
  5. Repeat for each remaining income source if you have more than one.

Spreadsheet Tax maps your file into HMRC update shapes. It does not replace your bookkeeping spreadsheet.

Year end

After quarterly updates, use Year end for annual adjustments, tax calculation, HMRC Assist, and the final declaration. Work through the checklist for each mirrored income source.

HMRC Assist feedback

HMRC Self Assessment Assist is a named step in Year end after Tax calculation. Messages are written by HMRC. Spreadsheet Tax only displays them and records that you confirmed you saw them.

  1. Open Year end and complete the questionnaire → checklist.
  2. Complete Tax calculation so HMRC has a calculation for the year.
  3. Open the HMRC Assist feedback step and choose Get HMRC Assist report.
  4. Read any messages HMRC returns. If there are none, HMRC returns no content (204) — that is normal.
  5. Confirm you have read the feedback so the report can be acknowledged with HMRC.

Requires an HMRC connection (individual) and Self Assessment Assist scopes on your authorisation. Feedback is only shown when HMRC returns it — Spreadsheet Tax does not invent Assist messages.

Common problems

  • Cannot load businesses — check National Insurance number in setup, then HMRC connection under Connect HMRC.
  • Upload rejected — fix the highlighted rows in your spreadsheet and upload again.
  • Authorisation expired — reconnect HMRC, then retry.
  • Submission history — open History for attempts and receipts.
